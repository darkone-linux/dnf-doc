My photos, my messages, my documents — my life, in short. Here, they are
protected **from the start**, without the slightest effort on my part. This page
explains, simply, why my system and my services are safer
than elsewhere. And why I can rest easy.

{/* t:p 39946a3d7d31b1f357f93715508cadedbf8067009eb663fe0bceb5cbf7aa80c3 */}
## A well-guarded house

My system is like a house. Security isn't a single big
lock&nbsp;: it's a series of protections that complement each other. If one fails,
the others hold.

<CardGrid>
  <Card title="A solid door" icon="seti:lock">
    A firewall filters what comes in. By default, **everything is closed** except what
    really needs to be open.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Keys that resist" icon="approve-check">
    My passwords are stored in a vault, in a form unreadable to
    a thief, even if they get their hands on the machine.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Separate rooms" icon="puzzle">
    Each service lives in its own room. An issue in one does not spread
    to the others.
  </Card>
  <Card title="An encrypted disk" icon="setting">
    If the hardware is stolen, the contents remain **undecipherable** without my key.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

{/* t:p 48fad7b6c72fd9cff17725aca88a0c5f904db58325a3a013a507a88296458fa6 */}
## Why it's safer than elsewhere

<CardGrid>
  <Card title="Official rules" icon="document">
    Hardening follows the recommendations of the **ANSSI**, the French
    national cybersecurity agency. The same requirements as for sensitive
    government agencies.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Transparent software" icon="seti:html">
    Everything is built on **free software**: its code is public, verifiable
    by anyone. Nothing is hidden, no backdoors.
  </Card>
  <Card title="On my machines, not elsewhere" icon="home">
    My data stays on **my machines**, not on the servers of a web giant
    that analyzes it to make a living.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Always up to date" icon="rocket">
    The system is kept **consistently** up to date: security
    patches arrive without breaking my workflow.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

## What this means for me

Good news&nbsp;: almost nothing changes day-to-day. Security works in the
**background**.

| I want to… | What happens |
|---|---|
| Log in | As usual, just a bit more secure |
| Use my services | Nothing changes, everything stays smooth |
| Choose a password | I'm asked to make it reasonably strong |
| Step away for a bit | The screen locks itself |

{/* t:p 6be6e5647d12551d20916ea28e7f6b85b64be49fa4899368642abaa45c5c17f9 */}
## My role, in three steps

The best lock is useless if I leave the key in the door.
A few simple habits are enough.

<CardGrid>
  <Card title="A good password" icon="star">
    Long and unique. An easy-to-remember phrase is better than a complicated word.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Lock the screen" icon="left-arrow">
    When leaving my desk, I lock it. The system also does it for me.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Ask when in doubt" icon="comment">
    A strange message or request&nbsp;? Better to ask my
    administrator than to click.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

## In summary

I don't have to become an expert to be well protected. The system handles it,
with **layered** protections, **official rules** and
**transparent software**. My data belongs to me, and it's in good
hands&nbsp;: my own.
